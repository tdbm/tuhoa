NATURAL AGRICULTURE

The improper application of pesticides by farmers (too high dosages, cocktailing of pesticides, inadequate pre-harvest intervals etc.) has a negative impact on environment and human health brought about by their toxic, particularly in more disadvantaged areas where farmers have to largely depend on cheap but often old and more toxic pesticides (Phan et al., 2014). In Liu et al's study (2014), high nitrate concentrations were found in the edible parts of leafy vegetables, probably harming human health if consumed. On the contrary, Masanobu Fukuoka's natural farming and permaculture has been expanded worldwide because his farming philosophy does not harm the environment and human health thanks to no usage of pesticides, NPK fertilizers & other chemicals, machine but the application of very little weeding (Korn, 2008). Part of his farming philosophy, particularly no use of pesticides has also been made in growing rice in An Giang, Vietnam, giving rise to significant achievements. Up to now, there have been 400 hectares of rice crops planted without any usage of pesticides in the whole province. Additionally, natural organic fertilizers especially created from the rice straw are to be employed nationwide in replacement for NPK fertilizers which are likely to damage the air, surface water, underground water, streams, rivers and even seas as concluded by various studies.

CLEAN WATER & SANITATION

2.2 - 5 million people around the world are annually reported to die from waterborne diseases, accounting for the fact that many areas in the world are facing a lack of clean water. And so is Vietnam. In the recent years, surface water, rice paddy field water and groundwater of many districts and cities of the provinces such as Ben Tre, Tien Giang, An Giang etc to the west of Saigon have been infected by saline, greatly impacting millions of people in these areas while various mountainous districts on High Lands are challenging long-time drought, resulting in a severe lack of clean water and water for their daily purposes.

The Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment (MNRE) state that approximately 80% of the diseases in Vietnam are caused by polluted water, probably giving rise to many cases of cholera, typhoid, dysentery and malaria each year (Suwal, 2010). The MNRE further claim that only 39% of the rural population has access to safe water and sanitation. For instance, many areas such as Phuoc Tien Hamlet, Bac Ai District, Ninh Thuan Province are severely lacking clean water and water for their daily activities. Follow us to the Stories Page to discover what has been going on to the hundreds of primary school children and teachers, who cannot wait for clean water and sanitation.
